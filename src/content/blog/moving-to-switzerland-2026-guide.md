## 1. The 2026 Regulatory Landscape: Permits & Quotas

The Swiss authorities have moved to a 100% digital "e-Permit" system in 2026, significantly speeding up the wait times for EU/EFTA nationals.

- **EU/EFTA**: The process is largely administrative. Registering at your local *Kreisbüro* or *Gemeinde* triggers your permit issue.
- **Non-EU (Third States)**: Quotas for L and B permits remain competitive. Large multinationals in Zurich and Basel continue to secure the majority of these spots.

## 2. Tax Reform: The End of the "Marriage Penalty"

The most significant change in 2026 is the voter-approved shift toward **Individual Taxation**.

Traditionally, married couples in Switzerland were taxed on their combined income, often pushing them into higher tax brackets. The 2026 reform begins the transition to taxing each individual separately, which is estimated to save double-income expat households between **5% and 12%** on their annual tax bill over the next decade.
