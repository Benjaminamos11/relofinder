Signing a lease for a beautiful apartment in Zurich or Geneva is a massive victory for any arriving expatriate. However, it is fundamentally just the halfway point of your transition.

What happens immediately after the ink dries on that rental contract is what distinguishes a stressful arrival from a seamless onboarding. This critical secondary phase is known within the industry as "Settling-In." 

If your HR department is handling your move, or if you are personally purchasing **Swiss relocation package inclusions**, it is vital to know exactly what occurs during this phase. Here is exactly what the best boutique agencies provide when executing elite **expat onboarding Switzerland**.

---

## Standard Inclusions: The Settling-In Checklist

A premium settling-in package transforms an empty Swiss apartment into a fully functional, legally compliant home. While massive global agencies often rush this step to meet volume KPIs, boutique Swiss agencies provide ongoing, highly localized, native-level support.

Here is the exact semantic checklist of standard inclusions you should expect:

<ul>
  <li><strong>1. Handover Protocol (Wohnungsübergabe):</strong> The single most critical step. Swiss landlords conduct brutally meticulous inspections of an apartment before handing over the keys. An expert relocation agent will attend this handover in person to actively negotiate and legally record pre-existing damages on the protocol, protecting your rental deposit (Mietkaution) from unfair deductions years later when you move out.</li>
  <li><strong>2. Internet & Utility Setup:</strong> Setting up electricity, water, and specialized high-speed Swiss internet (like Swisscom or Sunrise) requires native language skills to navigate the contracts and activation dates. A proper settling-in service handles all registrations so the Wi-Fi is active the minute you walk through the door.</li>
  <li><strong>3. Serafe (Swiss Radio/TV tax) Registration:</strong> Switzerland mandates a yearly household fee for radio and television access (Serafe), regardless of whether you own a TV. Failure to register results in sudden, aggressive billing and fines. Your agent will proactively handle this unglamorous registration.</li>
  <li><strong>4. Bank Account Setup:</strong> Due to stringent anti-money laundering regulations and FATCA rules (especially for US citizens), opening a Swiss bank account can be a nightmare of compliance. Relocation experts often have direct contacts at UBS, Credit Suisse (now UBS), or cantonal banks (ZKB) to expedite appointment booking and document clearance.</li>
  <li><strong>5. Driving License Exchange:</strong> Expats are typically required to physically exchange their foreign driving license for a Swiss license within the first 12 months. A full settling-in package includes instructions, translating requirements, and booking your appointment at the local *Strassenverkehrsamt*.</li>
</ul>

## Global Giants vs. Boutique Quality Assurance

It is crucial to understand that **Settling-in services Switzerland** are where boutique agencies truly shine compared to their global corporate competitors. 

Large-scale global agencies are highly incentivized to "close the file" the moment the lease is signed and the handover is finished. For them, settling-in is frequently a rushed, automated email checklist telling *you* where to call. 

Conversely, boutique Swiss agencies view settling-in as their core value proposition. Because they are local to the canton, they physically escort you to the bank, physically attend the handover, and actively advocate for you in Swiss German or French. This native-level buffering is what prevents profound expat burnout during the crucial first 30 days.

## A Major Warning: The 90-Day Deadline

One critical task often left to the expat is securing **[Swiss health insurance for expats](https://expat-savvy.ch/)**. You have exactly 3 months to choose a provider before cantonal authorities assign you to a costly default plan. 

While relocation agents assist intimately with utilities and banking, Swiss insurance is a highly personalized financial product that requires independent brokerage or direct procurement by the expat. Make this a priority the week you receive your residence permit.
