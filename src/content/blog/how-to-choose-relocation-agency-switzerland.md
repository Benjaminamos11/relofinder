# How to Choose a Relocation Agency in Switzerland (2026 Guide)

Switzerland's relocation industry is booming. With over 500 agencies operating across 26 cantons — from multinational firms with offices in 80 countries to one-person consultancies run from a Zurich apartment — the market has never been more fragmented. And that's exactly the problem.

If you're relocating to Switzerland in 2026, hiring the wrong agency can cost you thousands of francs, weeks of delays, and a mountain of stress. The right agency, on the other hand, can turn a six-month ordeal into a streamlined four-week process. They'll find you an apartment in a city with a 0.1% vacancy rate, navigate the labyrinth of Swiss permits, and ensure your kids have a school seat before your first day at work.

But how do you separate the professionals from the pretenders? This guide walks you through the types of agencies available, the exact criteria to evaluate, the red flags that should send you running, and how to use modern comparison tools to make a confident decision.

Whether you're a tech professional heading to [Zurich](/regions/zurich/), a family relocating to [Geneva](/regions/geneva/), or a remote worker considering the tax advantages of Zug, this guide will save you time, money, and frustration.

## The 3 Types of Swiss Relocation Agencies

Not all agencies serve the same clientele, and understanding the landscape is your first step toward making the right choice.

### 1. Global Corporate Agencies

**Examples**: Crown Relocations, SIRVA, Cartus, Santa Fe Relocation

**Price range**: CHF 8,000–15,000+ per relocation

Global agencies are the heavyweights. They operate in dozens of countries, manage thousands of relocations per year, and typically serve Fortune 500 companies with corporate relocation programs. If your employer is arranging your move and has a preferred vendor list, there's a good chance a global agency is on it.

**Pros:**
- Seamless international coordination — one point of contact from origin to destination
- Standardized processes with SLA guarantees
- Strong immigration teams with established relationships at cantonal migration offices
- Can handle complex scenarios: multi-country moves, lump-sum management, expense tracking for HR
- Often offer destination services, household goods shipping, and settling-in as a bundled package

**Cons:**
- You're often one of hundreds of active cases — personalization is limited
- Account managers may change mid-process
- Premium pricing — you're paying for global infrastructure even if you only need help in one city
- Services can feel formulaic; less flexibility for non-standard requests
- Some rely heavily on local subcontractors, adding a layer of communication

**Best for:** Large corporate moves with employer-funded relocation packages, executives, and multi-country relocations.

### 2. Swiss Boutique Agencies

**Examples**: Packimpex, Relocation Switzerland, FinGlobal, Living in Zug, various regional specialists

**Price range**: CHF 4,000–8,000 for individual/family relocations

Boutique agencies are the sweet spot for most expats relocating to Switzerland. They're locally owned, deeply connected to their regions, and offer the kind of hands-on service that global firms struggle to match. Many are run by former expats who went through the relocation process themselves.

**Pros:**
- Deep local knowledge — they know which landlords prefer expat tenants, which schools have open spots, and which neighborhoods match your lifestyle
- Personal relationships with cantonal authorities, real estate agents, and school administrators
- Flexible service packages — pay only for what you need
- Faster response times — your consultant is often the business owner
- Cultural sensitivity and integration support that goes beyond logistics

**Cons:**
- Limited to one or two cantons — if you're considering multiple Swiss locations, you may need different agencies
- Smaller teams mean capacity constraints during peak season (August–October)
- Quality varies widely — there's no universal accreditation standard
- May lack the corporate reporting infrastructure that HR departments require

**Best for:** Individual expats, families, and small-company relocations where personalized service matters more than global scale.

### 3. Digital/Hybrid Platforms

**Examples**: ReloFinder, Expatica services, various online immigration consultancies

**Price range**: CHF 1,500–3,000 for guided packages

The newest category in Swiss relocation is the digital-first platform. These combine technology (comparison tools, automated workflows, digital document management) with human expertise. Rather than assigning you a single consultant for everything, they connect you with specialists for each part of your relocation.

**Pros:**
- Most affordable entry point for budget-conscious expats
- Transparent pricing — you see exactly what you're paying for before committing
- Ability to compare multiple agencies and get competing quotes
- Self-service options for expats who want to handle parts of the relocation themselves
- Data-driven recommendations based on your profile, not just the agency's availability

**Cons:**
- Less hand-holding than traditional agencies — requires more self-direction
- Newer models with less track record than established firms
- May not cover every niche service (e.g., pet relocation, fine art shipping)

**Best for:** Budget-conscious expats, tech-savvy professionals, and those who want to mix self-service with professional help.

## 7 Things to Check Before Hiring an Agency

Regardless of which type of agency you're considering, these seven criteria will help you evaluate whether they're the right fit.

### 1. Regional Expertise

Switzerland is not one market — it's 26 cantonal markets, each with different rules, tax rates, housing dynamics, and cultural norms. An agency that excels in Geneva may be completely lost in Basel.

**What to check:**
- How many relocations has the agency completed in your target canton in the past 12 months?
- Do they have established relationships with the local Einwohnerkontrolle (residents' registration office) and migration office?
- Can they speak to specific neighborhood-level differences (e.g., the difference between Seefeld and Oerlikon in Zurich)?

If you're targeting Zurich, check our [Zurich region guide](/regions/zurich/) for what to expect. Heading to Geneva? Our [Geneva region guide](/regions/geneva/) covers the unique Romandie relocation landscape.

### 2. Service Coverage

"Relocation agency" can mean wildly different things. Some agencies focus exclusively on [immigration services](/services/immigration/) — work permits, residence permits, and visa applications. Others specialize in [housing search](/services/housing/) — apartment hunting, lease negotiation, and move-in support. The best agencies offer end-to-end coverage, but that comes at a premium.

**Core services to look for:**
- Immigration and permit assistance (B permit, L permit, C permit, cross-border G permit)
- Home search and lease negotiation
- School search and enrollment support
- Insurance setup (health, liability, household)
- Banking and financial onboarding
- Settling-in services (utilities, SBB, recycling, you name it)
- Cultural integration coaching

### 3. Pricing Transparency

This is where many agencies fall short. The Swiss relocation industry has no standardized pricing, which means two agencies offering seemingly identical services can charge wildly different amounts.

**What to demand:**
- A written quote with line-item pricing, not just a total
- Clear distinction between fixed fees and hourly rates
- Explicit list of what's included and what costs extra
- Payment schedule (upfront vs. milestone-based)
- Cancellation and refund terms

A typical breakdown might look like this:

| Service | Budget Range |
| :--- | :--- |
| Immigration/permit support | CHF 800–2,500 |
| Home search (3–5 viewings) | CHF 1,500–3,500 |
| School search | CHF 500–1,500 |
| Settling-in package | CHF 800–2,000 |
| Full-service bundle | CHF 4,000–8,000 |

For a complete cost breakdown, read our [relocation agency costs guide](/blog/relocation-agency-costs-switzerland-2026/).

### 4. Client Reviews and Track Record

Reviews are your most powerful tool — but they require careful reading. A 4.8-star rating on Google means little if the agency has only 6 reviews from two years ago. Look for volume, recency, and specificity.

**What to prioritize:**
- Agencies with 20+ Google reviews and a rating above 4.3
- Reviews that mention specific services (housing search, school placement, permit help)
- Reviews from the past 12 months — the industry changes fast
- Whether the agency responds to negative reviews (this shows accountability)

For deeper guidance, see our [agency reviews guide](/blog/best-relocation-agencies-switzerland-comparison/).

### 5. Response Time and Communication Style

During your relocation, you'll have urgent questions: your permit application needs an additional document, a landlord wants an answer within 24 hours, your child's school enrollment has a deadline. How quickly does the agency respond?

**Benchmark expectations:**
- Initial inquiry response: within 24 hours
- During active relocation: same-day responses for urgent matters
- Communication channels: email + phone + messaging (WhatsApp or similar)
- Dedicated contact person vs. team-based support

### 6. SARA Membership or EuRA Certification

Professional association membership isn't mandatory in Switzerland, but it's a strong trust signal. The two key bodies are:

- **SARA (Swiss Association of Relocation Agents)**: The national industry body. Members adhere to a code of conduct and meet quality standards.
- **EuRA (European Relocation Association)**: The pan-European accreditation body. EuRA Quality Seal holders have passed independent audits.

Neither guarantees perfection, but both indicate that the agency takes professionalism seriously.

### 7. Free Initial Consultation

Most reputable agencies offer a complimentary 30–60 minute consultation to understand your needs and propose a service plan. If an agency wants to charge you before even discussing your situation, consider that a warning sign.

**During the consultation, assess:**
- Do they ask detailed questions about your situation, or jump straight to selling packages?
- Can they explain the process clearly and set realistic timelines?
- Do they proactively mention potential challenges (e.g., Zurich's housing shortage)?
- Are they honest about what they can and can't help with?

## Red Flags to Watch Out For

After analyzing hundreds of agencies and thousands of reviews, these are the patterns that consistently signal trouble:

**No transparent pricing.** If an agency won't give you a written quote before you commit, walk away. Vague promises like "we'll figure it out as we go" often lead to surprise invoices of CHF 2,000–3,000 above the initial estimate.

**No verifiable reviews.** An agency with zero Google reviews, no LinkedIn presence, and no client testimonials is either brand new or actively avoiding public scrutiny. Neither is ideal when you're trusting them with a CHF 5,000+ investment.

**High-pressure sales tactics.** Reputable agencies don't pressure you to sign within 48 hours or claim their calendar is "almost full." The best agencies are confident enough in their service to let you take your time and compare options.

**No cancellation policy.** Life changes. Your start date might shift, your employer might alter the relocation package, or you might decide on a different city. A fair cancellation policy — with prorated refunds based on work completed — is standard practice. Agencies that demand full payment upfront with no refund clause are protecting themselves at your expense.

**Vague service descriptions.** "We'll help with everything" is not a service description. Demand specifics. How many apartment viewings are included? What happens if the first five apartments don't work out? Is school search a separate fee? Details matter.

**Subcontracting without disclosure.** Some agencies accept your contract and then pass the actual work to third-party contractors. This isn't inherently bad, but you should know about it. Ask directly: "Will you personally handle my relocation, or will parts be outsourced?"

## How to Compare Agencies Like a Pro

Finding the right agency isn't about picking the cheapest or the most expensive — it's about finding the best value for your specific situation. Here's a systematic approach:

**Step 1: Get at least 3 quotes.** Never commit to the first agency you speak with. Request detailed proposals from a minimum of three agencies, ideally mixing types (one global, one boutique, one digital).

**Step 2: Compare scope, not just price.** A CHF 4,000 quote that includes immigration, housing, and settling-in is better value than a CHF 3,000 quote that only covers housing. Create a simple spreadsheet to compare what's included.

**Step 3: Check canton-specific knowledge.** During your consultation call, ask specific questions about your target region. An agency that genuinely knows Zurich will talk about Kreis-level differences, the 1/3 rent-to-income rule, and the Betreibungsauskunft requirement. If they give generic answers, they're probably not specialists.

**Step 4: Use comparison tools.** Platforms like [ReloFinder](/companies/) aggregate agency profiles, reviews, and service details in one place. You can filter by region, service type, and price range to build a shortlist in minutes rather than days.

**Step 5: Check references.** Ask each agency for 2–3 recent client references. A confident agency will provide them without hesitation. When speaking to references, ask about communication responsiveness, whether the final cost matched the initial quote, and what they would have done differently.

## Boutique vs. Corporate — Which Is Right for You?

The global-vs-boutique decision depends on your specific circumstances. Here's a decision matrix to simplify the choice:

| Factor | Choose Global/Corporate | Choose Boutique |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget** | Employer-funded, CHF 10,000+ budget | Self-funded, CHF 3,000–8,000 budget |
| **Family size** | Large family, complex needs | Individual or small family |
| **Move complexity** | Multi-country, household goods shipping | Single-country, minimal belongings |
| **Employer involvement** | HR wants standardized reporting | No corporate oversight |
| **Timeline** | 3–6 months lead time | 4–8 weeks, need fast action |
| **Service style** | Process-driven, predictable | Personalized, flexible |
| **Target area** | Multiple cantons under consideration | One specific city or region |

### When Corporate Wins

Corporate agencies excel when the relocation involves significant logistical complexity: shipping a household across continents, managing tax equalization programs, coordinating with HR departments in multiple countries, or handling VIP/executive moves where service level agreements are contractually required.

If your employer is covering the cost and has a preferred supplier agreement, the global agency is typically the path of least resistance. The infrastructure is already in place, and your HR team will appreciate the standardized reporting.

### When Boutique Wins

For the vast majority of individual expats and families moving to Switzerland, a boutique agency will deliver better outcomes at a lower price. The difference is most dramatic in housing search — a boutique agency with deep local connections can access off-market listings that global agencies simply don't see.

Boutique also wins when cultural integration matters to you. A locally owned agency that employs former expats understands the emotional side of relocation, not just the logistical side. They'll tell you which neighborhood has the best Spielgruppe for your toddler, which Gemeinde processes permits fastest, and which health insurance broker actually answers the phone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a relocation agency cost in Switzerland?

Costs typically range from CHF 2,000 for basic packages to CHF 15,000+ for full-service corporate relocations. Boutique agencies average CHF 4,000–8,000 for individual relocations. The final price depends on the services included, your family size, and the complexity of your move. For a detailed breakdown, read our [relocation costs guide](/blog/relocation-agency-costs-switzerland-2026/).

### Should I use a global or boutique relocation agency?

Global agencies like Crown and SIRVA suit large corporate moves with international coordination needs. Boutique Swiss agencies offer personalized, local expertise and are often better for individual or family relocations. The right choice depends on your budget, employer involvement, and how important hands-on service is to you.

### What questions should I ask a relocation agency before hiring?

Ask about their experience in your target canton, pricing transparency, what's included vs. extra, their response times, client references, and whether they offer a free initial consultation.
