## The Swiss Health Insurance Problem

Every resident in Switzerland must choose mandatory basic health insurance (KVG/LAMal) within three months of arrival. The coverage is legally standardized, but premiums still vary by canton, age, deductible, insurance model, and provider.

For newcomers, the hard part is not just finding the cheapest premium. The bigger risk is choosing a setup that ignores supplementary insurance (VVG), private or semi-private hospital cover, accident coverage, family protection, or Pillar 3a planning.

That is why a good consultant should help with three questions:

- Which KVG model and franchise fit my medical usage and liquidity?
- Should I apply for supplementary cover before health questions become harder?
- How does health insurance connect with liability, family protection, hospital cover, and Vorsorge?

## Common Questions

### Do I need a broker for Swiss health insurance?

Not always. Basic KVG coverage is standardized, so a healthy single person may be able to compare premiums online. A broker or consultant becomes more useful when you have a family, supplementary insurance, hospital-cover preferences, pre-existing conditions, or pension-planning questions.

### Is the cheapest KVG plan always best?

No. The legal benefits are the same, but service model, provider administration, doctor-access model, deductible, accident coverage, and supplementary strategy still matter.

### When should I review supplementary insurance?

Before cancelling or switching anything. Supplementary insurance is medically underwritten, so changing too quickly can damage old rights or lead to exclusions.
