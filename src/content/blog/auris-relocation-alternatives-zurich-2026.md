## 📋 Who is Auris Relocation?

Auris Relocation AG is a Swiss-owned global mobility firm and the largest privately held independent in the country. Founded in Zurich, Auris is **EuRA-accredited**, holds **ISO 27001** certification for data security, and was awarded the **EcoVadis Silver Medal in March 2025**, placing it in the top 15% of all firms assessed worldwide on sustainability.

Their service catalogue covers the full mobility lifecycle:

- Immigration advisory (work permits, residence permits, family registration)
- Home search and lease negotiation
- Cross-cultural and language training
- Settling-in support (banking, schools, insurance setup)
- Corporate moving management (one-off and recurring assignment programmes)
- Departure services for expats leaving Switzerland

**Best For:** Mid-to-large multinationals running structured assignment programmes who value a single national vendor with multi-canton coverage and documented compliance credentials.

### 1. Anchor Relocation
**★ 4.9/5 (ReloFinder Verified — family & schools)**

**Best For:** Senior executives and families relocating with school-age children, where the home search must align with a specific international school catchment or commuter pattern.

Anchor has 20+ years in Zurich and Central Switzerland and is the agency most often cited in our exit interviews for meticulous family-and-school orientation. ### 3. Lodge Relocation
**★ 4.7/5 (ReloFinder Verified — pan-Swiss multilingual coverage)**

**Best For:** Assignments that span more than one canton or language region — Zurich-Geneva rotations, Basel-Zug splits, or any move where the destination canton is not yet final at signing.

Lodge Relocation has been active in Switzerland since 1994 and is the most established boutique with genuine nationwide infrastructure. Their own offices in Zurich, Zug, Basel, Geneva, Lausanne, Neuchâtel, Fribourg, and Lugano give them a single coordination layer across all three Swiss language regions — something most competitors achieve only through subcontracting. For families or executives whose move may shift between French- and German-speaking Switzerland, Lodge is the safest single-vendor choice.

**Services:** Home search, settling-in, school orientation, departure services, multilingual destination services, corporate relocation programmes

**Coverage:** All Swiss cantons (German, French, Italian-speaking)

**Good to know:** Lodge's edge is the linguistic and regional handover. If your role splits between Zurich and Geneva, or you need the same consultant team handling a German- and a French-speaking case, Lodge is the only boutique-tier option that does not switch you to a partner agency at the language border.

## ✅ How to Choose Between Auris and the Alternatives

The decision depends on three variables: who is paying, what district you are targeting, and whether your move is structured or improvised. Use the decision tree below.

**Choose Auris Relocation if:**

- Your employer runs a structured assignment programme and wants a single Swiss vendor across multiple cantons
- Compliance credentials (ISO 27001, EcoVadis) matter to your HR or risk team
- You value depth of corporate process over a single owner-operator relationship

**Choose Anchor Relocation if:**

- You are moving with school-age children and the school decision drives the housing decision
- You want boutique-style attention but corporate-level breadth
- You are relocating into Zurich Districts 7 or 8, or Central Switzerland

**Choose Prime Relocation if:**

- You are targeting Zurich Seefeld/Enge, central Zug, or the Goldküste
- You have a tight move-in deadline (under 6 weeks)
- Off-market access matters more to you than ancillary services

**Choose Lodge Relocation if:**

- Your assignment may shift between cantons or language regions during the move
- You want one consistent team handling Zurich-Geneva, Basel-Zug, or German-to-French rotations
- You value a 30-year nationwide track record over local-only depth

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### What does Auris Relocation actually do?

Auris Relocation is a Swiss-owned, EuRA-accredited mobility firm offering immigration advisory, home search, cross-cultural training, settling-in support, and corporate moving management across seven Swiss cities. It is the largest privately held independent in the country and one of the few firms holding ISO 27001 and EcoVadis Silver credentials. For a side-by-side view of how it stacks up against boutique competitors, see our [Switzerland-wide agency comparison](/blog/best-relocation-companies-switzerland-2026/).

### How much does Auris Relocation cost in 2026?

Auris quotes per assignment and does not publish public rate cards. ReloFinder's 2026 audit of comparable boutique and mid-market providers in Zurich found CHF 4,000-8,000 for selective home-search packages and CHF 6,000-12,000 for full-service relocation. Family-of-four moves typically land at CHF 8,000-13,000. [Get free competing quotes here](/contact/) before signing.

### Why do expats look for Auris alternatives?

The most common reasons are wanting a more boutique owner-operator relationship rather than a corporate consultant pool, needing specialist off-market access in 0.1% vacancy districts, or covering a move that spans multiple cantons or language regions. Anchor Relocation, Prime Relocation, and Lodge Relocation each address one of these three gaps — see the comparison table above.

### Which Auris alternative is best for off-market apartments in Zurich?

**Prime Relocation** is the strongest off-market specialist in Zurich and Zug. Their landlord network in Seefeld, Enge, Wollishofen, and central Zug is the differentiator. Pair with [Offlist.ch](https://offlist.ch) for parallel signal to landlords.

### Is Anchor Relocation cheaper than Auris?

Both target the boutique-to-mid market and pricing depends on scope. ReloFinder's audit places typical Anchor packages at CHF 5,000-10,000 for full-service moves to Zurich or Zug — broadly comparable to Auris but with more flexible modular billing.

### Does Auris handle work permits?

Yes. As an EuRA member with multi-canton presence, Auris provides immigration advisory and registration support for both EU and non-EU nationals. Complex non-EU B-permit cases often benefit from a parallel second opinion from a permit specialist — many ReloFinder users add [Expat-Services.ch](https://expat-services.ch) for this layer.

### How long does relocation to Zurich actually take in 2026?

Plan a 10-14 week runway from offer-letter to keys-in-hand. The home-search phase has compressed to 3-6 weeks given listings now clear in 16-18 days, but non-EU permit timelines still add 4-12 weeks. A good agency does these in parallel; an under-resourced one does them in sequence.
