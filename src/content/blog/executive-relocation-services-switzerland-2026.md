## 📋 What Executive Relocation Actually Means in 2026

Executive relocation is the white-glove tier of corporate mobility, reserved for C-suite, senior VP, GM and specialist hires whose offer letters justify a CHF 50,000-80,000+ programme spend. The defining features are a 1:5 consultant-to-client ratio, off-market housing access, expedited cantonal permit handling, partner career support, and a single accountable consultant from acceptance to day-90.

Standard corporate relocation, by contrast, runs a 1:15 ratio, relies on public listing platforms, and follows ordinary cantonal permit timelines. The price difference is roughly 2-3x, but the placement-success and retention impact compounds — Switzerland's 2026 unemployment rate of around 3.1% means executives have options if the move sours.

## ⚠️ Where Standard Corporate Relocation Falls Short for Executives

Executive moves fail in predictable places when handled by a standard tier consultant. The five most common failure modes from ReloFinder's 2026 audit:

1. **Housing window misses.** Zurich and Geneva vacancy rates sit near 0.1%. Listings disappear in 24-48 hours and landlords receive 50-100 applications per apartment. Standard tier consultants chase public platforms; executive desks work landlord networks.
2. **Permit quota timing.** Switzerland's 2026 quotas — 4,500 B permits and 4,000 L permits for non-EU nationals — were 52% used by end of September 2025. Zurich, Zug and Geneva exhaust theirs first. Executive desks file early and use cantonal short-term quotas to bridge.
3. **School placement waitlists.** Top international schools in Zurich and Geneva run 12-18 month waitlists for popular grades. Executive desks have admissions-office relationships; standard consultants submit and wait.
4. **Spousal career neglect.** Roughly half of executive transfer failures trace to a partner who couldn't rebuild a career. Executive packages include partner support; standard packages do not.
5. **Tax briefing timing.** Quellensteuer (withholding tax), pillar 3a optimisation and Lump-sum Taxation eligibility for high-income foreigners need briefing *before* the contract is signed, not after. See the [Pillar 3a guide](https://expat-savvy.ch/3rd-pillar/) for the personal-finance layer.
